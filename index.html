Shivani Sakri

I build physics-based models of vehicles that fly, float, and hover — then take them to the field and find out where the model is wrong.

Five years of vehicle-dynamics modeling, external-flow CFD, and flight- and sea-trial data validation, mostly on craft that don't fit neatly into either aero or hydro. My dissertation is on the nonlinear dynamics and stability of single-propeller air cushion vehicles, funded by the Office of Naval Research.

Dissertation

Hill-climbing air cushion vehicle

Lead engineer on a multi-year program to get a skirted hovercraft up a 35° slope. I own the whole loop: a 5-DOF Simulink model coupling aerodynamic forces, propulsion airflow, and skirt–ground interaction; Star-CCM+ CFD to feed it parameters; a ROS data-acquisition system on the prototype; and field tests that tell me which parts of the model were fiction.

Full-scale seaglider performance analysis

At Regent Craft I built the foundational Python performance-analysis codebase for a vehicle class nobody had modeled before: ingest raw flight and sea-trial telemetry, compute aero/hydrodynamic coefficients, produce performance-envelope diagnostics. Then calibrated the physics model against HIL and sea-trial data and designed validation tests to isolate specific error modes.

Hydrofoil craft performance model

Newest ONR project. Formulating a 2-DOF physics-based hydrodynamic performance model and building a custom MATLAB simulator around it for dynamics, control response, and stability analysis — the same modeling discipline as the hovercraft work, applied to a foil-borne craft.

Hoverski — jetski to hovercraft

Converting a jetski into a hovercraft: skirt, lift system, and a MATLAB/Simulink lift and cushion model to size it. A hands-on counterweight to the analysis work — the fastest way I know to learn what a cushion actually does is to build one and watch it misbehave.
