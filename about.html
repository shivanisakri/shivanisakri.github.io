About

Shivani Sakri

I'm an Aerospace Engineering Ph.D. candidate at Virginia Tech, with a concurrent M.S. in Ocean Engineering — a combination that sounds odd until you look at what I work on. Air cushion vehicles, seagliders, and hydrofoils all live at the interface between air and water, and they punish you for treating either one as an afterthought.

My research is on the nonlinear dynamics and stability of single-propeller air cushion vehicles, in the VT iShip Lab at the Center for Marine Autonomy and Robotics, advised by Prof. Stefano Brizzolara and funded by the Office of Naval Research. Day to day that means Simulink models, Star-CCM+ runs, a ROS data-acquisition system on a prototype, and field tests that regularly tell me I was wrong about something.

What I've come to care about is the loop between the two: a model that never meets hardware is a hypothesis, and test data with no model behind it is just a spreadsheet. The work I want to keep doing is the part in the middle — designing the test that isolates the error, finding the parameter that was lying, and closing the gap.

Before this I did an M.S. at Michigan and a mechanical engineering degree in Pune, and spent a summer at Regent Craft doing performance analysis on a full-scale hybrid seaglider. I'm currently finishing my Ph.D. and looking for flight test, simulation, and vehicle-physics roles.
